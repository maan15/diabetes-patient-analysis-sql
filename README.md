**📌 Problem Statement**
**Analyze patient health data to identify key factors contributing to diabetes and detect high-risk individuals using SQL.**

**🛠 Tools Used**
-SQL (MySQL/PostgreSQL)

-Data Analysis Techniques (Aggregation, Window Functions, Views)

**📊 Analysis Performed**
-Identified patients with above-average BMI and glucose levels
-Categorized patients based on BMI using CASE statements
-Ranked patients by glucose levels using window functions
-Created views to identify high-risk patients
-Analyzed blood pressure across diabetic and non-diabetic groups
-Segmented patients based on age groups

**🔍 Key Insights**
Patients with high BMI (>30) and high glucose levels are at significantly higher risk of diabetes
Diabetic patients have higher average glucose and BMI compared to non-diabetic patients
Inactive metabolic indicators like high insulin variance highlight risk clusters
Majority of high-risk patients fall in the 30–50 age group
Strong relationship observed between glucose levels and diabetes outcome
